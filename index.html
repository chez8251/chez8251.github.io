Introduction

The revolutionary that brought us genius products of multiple board sportswear, items and the CEO of the powerhouse Quiksilver, Andy Mooney, has allowed us to interview him on the essence of surf wear and his ideals with his company along with the terminating the 23 year old sponsorship with the beloved champion surfer, Kelly Slater.
Quiksilver was founded in 1969 intended to be an apparel and surf wear shop. It has since grown substantially having a net income of a whopping 82.3 billion and having shops located all around the world. Quiksilver has also spawned two more sister shops targeted for women called Roxy and DC Shoes.

Tell us what you envision as the perfect fashion for sports?

When I first got the position of CEO of Quiksilver, I set out to make that ‘perfect fashion’ for sportswear. Unfortunately, I realized that wasn’t the case. Different sports require different specifications with the apparel. It’s amazing how different your performance in the particular sport due to the clothes you are wearing. My advice would be to wear the comfiest clothes that you can get your hands on that is fitting for that sport and just wear it. Quiksilver offers a variety of very fashionable, comfy and practical clothes.

What is your dream of Quiksilver’s future?

I like to think that Quiksilver’s path will be to grow even further and to branch out into different areas. Of course, I am edging it towards that path but who knows what the future brings?
